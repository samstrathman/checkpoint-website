with classical music, then moved towards more rock and progressive metal, and ended up at fingerstyle acoustic music. video games have always been a huge part of my life. i was a big Nintendo fanboy growing up and when i was 13 i discovered this little game that was still in beta: minecraft. i poured so many hours into that game, and it lead me down the rabbit hole of indie games. i have so much respect for indie game devs, they showed me that you don't need a massive team to make something, you just need some drive and time and a ton of patience. they also showed me that you don't need to be and man the artists that i discovered through indie games are absolutely top notch. people like C418 (minecraft), Disasterpeace (fez), Scott Lloyd Shelly (Terraria), Austin Wintory (Journey). i can't overstate how important these musicians were to my child self. at around 14 or 15 i started writing music. at that point i was really into quiet ambient stuff (probably because i was playing so much minecraft at the time), so that's what i started writing. piano music, ironically. it's still some of the most meaningful stuff to me. i started a music project called Desosign and over the years i tried out a bunch of different genres, which you can check out here. that music is okay, some of it i'm proud of some of it not so much, but it was just nice to have a project like that where i could kind of try out whatever i wanted. in 2020 however i decided that i wanted a music project that was more cohesive, more polished, and something that was as true to me as possible, something that would combine my love for video games and game soundtracks with the guitar and production skills i had built up over the years, and thus checkpoint was born. i spent the next year learning basic 3d art and animation with blender and writing checkpoint's debut ep, and i'm super proud of it. i'm nearly done with my next release, so stay tuned!

i'm a huge supporter of indie games and it's been a dream of mine to write the soundtrack a game, so if you're looking for someone send me a message through the contact section!
